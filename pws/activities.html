Writing Activities

Writing Prompts

Use these open-ended ideas to spark your creativity and dive into a story, scene, or character:

  • “You receive a letter in the mail with no return address. Inside is a single sentence: ‘Meet me where it all began.’”
  • “Describe a town where no one ever sleeps.”
  • “Your reflection starts doing things you’re not.”

Writing Challenges

Try these mini-challenges to push your creativity and build writing stamina:

  • Write a story without using the letter “e.”
  • Write a complete scene using only dialogue.
  • Write 100 words a day for a week, no excuses.

Show, Don’t Tell

Practice describing emotions and actions through sensory details and behavior instead of directly stating them:

  • Instead of “He was angry,” try “His fists clenched as his jaw tightened.”
  • Describe fear without using the words scared, afraid, or terrified.
  • Write a character’s sadness through their environment—what do they notice, what do they ignore?

Story Starters

Begin your story using one of these openers and see where your imagination takes you:

  • “The storm didn’t knock, it barged in.”
  • “Everyone knew not to go past the old gate… except me.”
  • “I woke up with a map drawn on my arm and no memory of how it got there.”
