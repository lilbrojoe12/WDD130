Overview

Purpose

Our purpose is to help writers find tips and advice they need to get started on their writing projects. Write to Create can help them learn how best to put their ideas on paper and how to really draw in their readers.

Audience

My intended audience is novice writers of all ages, particularly those aspiring to become better at creative writing. I'd like them to visit my website and gain the knowledge they need to successfully write creatively.

Content

Home page

Why Write to Create? Throughout the world, there are a lot of people with aspirations to create in some way. Like me, a portion of those individuals have desires to pursue creative writing, whether it be in the form of a simple hobby or a legitimate career. So why would they come to my website, Write to Create? Write to Create is made for novice writers, those at a beginner level who simply want to learn the basic fundamentals and best practices for writing creatively. Upon visiting Write to Create, these aspiring writers will be able to discover new techniques, as well as touch up on their grammar and simple writing skills.

What to do? Whenever visitors come to Write to Create, some activities they may be able to complete include viewing blogs with tips and advice for writing, viewing frequently asked questions where they can see what questions people have had, and participating in a variety of writing activities such as daily writing prompts, mad libs, and mini writing challenges. All of these things will be helpful for them in their writing journey, and they’ll be able to get a good start becoming creative writers.

Why not another website? There are probably several websites that have content similar to what is found on Write to Create. However, in my own experience, there aren’t very many websites that are specifically geared towards creative writing. Visitors will come to Write to Create because its main focus is creative writing, particularly for those who don’t have much experience with creative writing and would like to develop skills so they may achieve their personal writing goals. It will be user friendly and easy to navigate, and it won’t lead novices away by belittling them in any way. It will be an encouraging website with the attitude of helping writers gain the confidence they need to achieve their goals.

Frequently Asked Questions The Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) section will probably be located close to the bottom of the home page. People always have questions, and they often want answers to those questions without having to search for a lot of time. In the Write to Create Frequently Asked Questions section, visitors will be able to see a lot of common questions that other people have had and be able to get a better understanding of whatever they’re asking.

Blog

Blogs The blogs page will be where visitors to Write to Create will learn the most about writing tips and advice. Most of the skills, suggestions, and advice presented in the blogs will be things that are easy to learn and put into practice. The blog posts will also not be so long that visitors will get bored and give up on Write to Create. They’ll be shorter and to the point so readers get good information in not a lot of content.

What our visitors will learn There are several questions Write to Create will be able to answer for its visitors. As I’ve already discussed, those visiting write to create will be able to learn good habits for writing creatively. Not only that, but they’ll be able to look at examples of what has worked for other novice writers to draw in their audience.

Writing Activities

Writing Activities The Writing Activities page will have a variety of different things writers can do to practice their writing skills. They probably won’t be able to interact with it specifically, but it will give them things they can do own their own, depending on what the activity is. The activities will include daily writing prompts. This will be where they can get a prompt to use as they practice writing a short story. Another activity will include a mini writing challenge, where they can test their skills by completing challenges. An example may be to write a story in only 6 words. Other activities besides these will be available for them to try.
